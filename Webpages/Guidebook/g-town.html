Locations

Town

Population: 998

The residents have built upon the skeleton of Town to form functional community, albeit one without some of the modern amenities residents had become accustomed to. Town sits in a valley with arable land and is surrounded on four sides by the mountainous woodlands known as The Blank. Town consists of two areas: The Residences, where residents live, and Main Street, a series of shops, governmental buildings and the Town Square.

Notable Main Street locations:

  • Town Square: The center of main street, the Town Square is a lively public space with benches and shady trees. A major source of activity in Town. Later in the day into the evening, Town Square may be filled with vendors trading and bartering, street buskers and some Council-sanctioned events.
  • Town Hall: The only two-story building in Home. The Town Hall houses The council room, offices for all of Town Hall's employees including those of the councilors, the sheriff, and the deputies, a small jail with two cells, the record room and a large atrium that functions as a communal meeting space.
  • The Clinic: A rudimentary clinic, with basic tools.
  • The Morgue: (New as of Aug 1, 2023). A small building located to the left of Town Hall, the morgue consists of an autopsy table and cadaver storage, with rudimentary tools. The morgue is monitored by Town's part-time coroner, Valerian Ellis.
  • The Distillery: An intimate bar and distillery run by Councilman Clay. The Distillery opened in June, and has been providing residences with liquor of questionable quality. Currently, the supplies for mash have been supplied by The Watcher, but The Distillery has had to occasionally close after selling out of liquor in order to wait for mash ingredients to periodically restock. The Distillery has a small stage for local performers.

Notable Residences locations:

  • The Hanging Gardens: A block of 18 houses to the south of Town Hall, bordering No Man's Land, with their siding and doors decorated with painted green vines and various flowers. These are the pleasure houses in Town, the Red Light District, full of a mixture of pleasures and vices that can be found in Home, ranging from sex work to psychedelic research. Also home of artisans and craftspeople who can work with the materials that Cheyenne Davis can transmutate and work with her on her projects.
  • Le Restaurant: (New as of Sep 1, 2023) A house in No-Man's Land which has had the bedroom furniture removed and replaced with 6 indoor dining tables and 4 outdoor dining tables. When it's nice out, more of the tables are moved outside. Each table is decorated with chamomile flowers and a wooden board menu, with the available dishes designated with calligraphy. The restaurant is marked by a wooden sign hanging to the top right of the door that says 'Le Restaurant'.

Notable locations outside of Town:

  • Memorial Cemetery Park: A park erected in The Meadows after the slaughter on the night of the Masquerade Ball. Consists of 33 grave sites that residents may visit to mourn, or to celebrate life.
